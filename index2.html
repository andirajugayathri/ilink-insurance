Frequently Asked Questions

1. Why is insurance important or necessary?
  • The uncertainty of business losses is reduced when you have your insurance plan in place. The coverage helps you to be prepared, prevent, safeguard, or manage when uncertainties occur.
  • Insurance can help in maintaining the welfare of your employees, where manpower is a strength to your business.
  • Professionally handle business interruption scenarios and cover your business to continue even in times of crisis.
  • While your insurance brokers protect your business with industry-specific insurance solutions covering potential risks, you can focus on your business with more confidence.
2. Are insurance brokers or agents worth it?
Getting the right insurance policy is as important for your business as having one. Insurance brokers are trained to understand and tailor policies for multiple business scenarios across different sectors, benefiting the customer. They provide access to the best insurers in Australia, making the process easier and stress-free.

It is worthwhile to seek the help of an expert when it comes to insurance, to safeguard a business that you have invested so much in. From comparing quotes, getting policies at the best rates, payment options, friendly alerts on policy renewals, to backing you up with industry-specific knowledge for claims, insurance brokers simplify the process for you.
3. How do insurance companies work?
Insurance is a financial product sold by insurance companies that safeguards you and your business against various business-associated risks for insured events. You pay premiums, and in return, the companies offer risk coverage for specific events. Insurance brokers, with their expertise, help tailor policies that suit your business's needs and negotiate the best deals.

You should buy insurance, especially when:
  • It is required by law, such as liability insurance.
  • You have invested significantly, and a financial loss/claim could exceed your capacity to recover.
  • Your business faces higher risk exposure.
4. How are insurance premiums calculated?
Typically insurance premiums are calculated based on the type of business, size, turnover, type of cover needed, type and duration of the policy, level of coverage, payment cycles, and a whole lot of scenarios that are unique and specific to your industry and your business situation.
